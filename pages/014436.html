I think you may be interested in this...?

This post - We're Playing Your Song: Personalised Track Notifications over XMPP just caught my on on the BBC Radio labs blog: "Just a little XMPP toy: send the message lastfm yourlastfmusername to radio1@hug.hellomatty.com and we'll ping you when one of our music stations is playing a tune by an artist you like to listen to. Fun, huh?"

It goes on: "Behind the scenes I'm pulling your last.fm profile via tastebroker.org which wrangles it into APML. This means I'm storing your preferences in a way that should work as well for speech shows as it does for music. So I could use tastebroker's view of your del.icio.us feed to let you know when Radio 4 is broadcasting something you might be interested in. But that's for another time..."

A quick visit to tastebroker reveals that it can prduce APML from last.fm, Pandora and delicious... the delicious generated APML from my delicious account is a weighted list of tags I have used, as far as I can tell...

Anyway, some time ago Martin was "imagin[ing] what would education (or even just a course) be like if it was built around something like Last.fm, would it be a Last.uni?". Maybe this is another step along the road?

Just by the by, I wonder - is there a simple hack to use a transformed APML file as a generator for Google custom search engine definition file, or maybe a beter question is, what's the best way of using an APML file to seed a Google CSE?
